GeorgeWJ 11-Oct-18 11:49 PM
Okay, so I was thinking about what Dan had said about our game: Keeping the player engaged. Basically, the issue is keeping players interacting with our game. Our core gameloop dictates that players see a tree and then takes action (by tapping on it). That tree is then turned into logs which builds tension, before becoming a house which is the big pay off. The issue here is that players are being rewarded just for tapping on the screen. We are expecting our players to just constantly tap away, right? It just isn't very engaging at all, and I'm certain that the game will get old very quickly. So I was looking at other management/creature sim games and found that it's not that performing actions repeatedly keep players engaged, its performing different variations of said actions which lead to surprising results that keeps players engaged. So how could we use this information to improve our game? I think that instead of pleasing the islands creatures, we should instead attract different kinds of creatures, and treat the island as a personal garden. We could make seeds produce different kinds of trees which then attract different kinds of creatures for example? Then our game becomes about collecting a set of creatures. We have a definitive goal for the Player at this point, where they will interact with the environment to gain specific items, use them to shape the environment, be rewarded with new creatures and then be able to gather more exotic tools/items to repeat the cycle. What do you guys think?
